## Title
N-body dynamics of Topology-Generated Potential: Earnshaw violation, chaos suppression, and the EFT origin of Yukawa coupling

## Authors
Mateusz Serafin

## Abstract
We derive and verify numerically the complete N-body dynamics arising from the TGP scalar-field Lagrangian with quartic self-interaction. The potential decomposes as V = V_2 + V_3^{irr}, where V_2 is a closed-form pairwise interaction (gradient + repulsive + confining) and V_3 is an irreducible three-body term computed via a Feynman two-parameter integral.

Eight principal results emerge. (1) TGP breaks the Earnshaw theorem: equilateral three-body configurations admit stable static equilibria with all normal modes positive. (2) TGP suppresses three-body chaos by 35-45% (measured via the maximal Lyapunov exponent). (3) The Yukawa source coupling C_eff is derived from EFT projection of the classical topological defect. (4) Classical soliton interaction is not Yukawa (oscillatory, power-law decay), establishing that the Yukawa law requires the EFT mass gap. (5) The irreducible three-body integral admits a semi-analytic multipole representation with controlled accuracy (<0.01% at L_max=15). (6) The ratio |V_3/V_2| is mapped across the dimensionless parameter t = m_sp d, showing perturbative behaviour (~1%) in the Lyapunov regime. (7) Closed-form expressions for equilibrium distances, normal-mode frequencies, and the TGP Hill sphere radius are derived. (8) For unequal masses, the phase diagram is governed by a critical coupling beta_crit; the escape velocity and a three-body breathing mode are derived in closed form.

All results are supported by a regression suite of 59 numerical tests (10/10 reproduction stages PASS in ~100s).
