\begin{abstract}
Topology-Generated Potential (TGP) is a scalar-field theory in which
\textbf{space is not a fixed background} but is generated by the presence
of mass and energy.  Each fundamental particle produces a local
contribution to a shared field $\Phi$, whose value measures the ``density
of generated space.''  Nonlinear interference of multiple sources produces
three distinct regimes of interaction: (i)~long-range attraction
(gravity), (ii)~intermediate-range repulsion, and (iii)~short-range
confinement.  The fundamental constants $c$, $\hbar$, $G$ are
field-dependent, with the Planck length as the sole invariant.

The theory yields an exponential metric that reproduces all 10 PPN
parameters of GR exactly ($\gamma = \beta = 1$), predicts
$c_{\mathrm{GW}} = c_0$ identically (consistent with GW170817), and
contains $\Lambda$CDM as a smooth limit ($\Delta\chi^2 = +1.8$,
$\Delta\mathrm{AIC} = +3.8$ vs.\ $\Lambda$CDM on Planck\,+\,DESI data).
Gravitational lensing is identical to GR in the weak field; deviations
appear only in the strong-field regime (BH shadow $\sim$37\% smaller).
In the particle sector, a single substrate parameter $g_0^e = 0.869$
yields lepton mass ratios to 0.001\% and $\alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.1174$
($0.6\sigma$ from PDG) with zero free parameters.

This document is a self-contained executive summary of the full
410-page manuscript.  Detailed derivations, proofs, and numerical
verifications are available in the companion repository.
\end{abstract}
