## §2 — Definition: Fractional Flux String (FFS)

### §2.1 — Operational definition

W TGP S05:
$$
\Phi(x) = \rho(x) \cdot e^{i\theta(x)}, \qquad \theta(x) \in U(1)
$$

**Standard integer winding** (closed loop in 3D):
$$
\oint_\gamma d\theta = 2\pi n, \quad n \in \mathbb{Z}
$$

**FFS — fractional winding on OPEN string:**

A 1-dimensional defect line (cosmic string-like) w polu Φ, connecting **two point singularities** of $\rho$, z winding $q = m/N$ ($N \in \mathbb{Z}_+$, $m \in \mathbb{Z}$):
$$
\oint_{\gamma_\perp} d\theta = 2\pi q
$$

dla loop $\gamma_\perp$ przecinającego linię. **Klucz strukturalny:** the line is **physical object** (not pure topology) carrying flux $q$.

### §2.2 — Endpoint singularity = kwark

**Kwark** = endpoint of fractional flux string. Each endpoint:
- Local point gdzie $\rho \to 0$ (string core terminates)
- Sends out FFS z winding $q$
- **Single endpoint cannot exist stably:** string must terminate somewhere; isolated endpoint → infinite string energy → "rozplątuje się"
- Stabilization: **another endpoint** (other quark, or vertex)

To kwantyfikuje user's intuition (§3.1 dialog):

> *"To coś jak węzeł który dąży do rozplątania, i sam by to zrobił ale w obecności innych nie może."*

### §2.3 — Closure topologies (meson + baryon)

**Meson:** 2 endpointy connected by single FFS:
```
q --- FFS(winding +q) --- q̄
```
Z $q + (-q) = 0$ integer → closure consistency. **Stable.**

**Baryon:** 3 endpointy at Y-vertex z FFSes:
```
        q1
        |
       FFS(winding q1)
        |
   Y-VERTEX
       /  \
  FFS(q2) FFS(q3)
     /        \
    q2        q3
```
Z $q_1 + q_2 + q_3 = $ integer at vertex (Kirchhoff-like consistency).

**Concrete fractional choice:** $q \in \{+2/3, -1/3\}$ z proton $u_R u_G d_B$ (windings $2/3 + 2/3 - 1/3 = 1$) ✓; neutron $u d d$ (windings $2/3 - 1/3 - 1/3 = 0$) ✓.
