# OP-6 / M3-c — Scaling dimensions at 3D Ising Wilson–Fisher

**Status:** working, 2026-04-24.
**Predecessors:** `M2a_analytical_sketch.md`, `M2c_H3_reality_check.md`.
**Constraint:** preserve Z₂-odd microscopic field ŝ; minimal modifications
of H₁ allowed.
**Question:** does H₁ (or a minimal Z₂-symmetric extension) generate the
H_GL kinetic functional `(K_geo/2) φ⁴ (∇φ)²` as an IR effective action
under RG at the 3D Ising Wilson–Fisher fixed point?

---

## 1. Effective-theory ansatz

Under block-RG from scale Λ (UV) to scale k (IR), the generating
functional for the Z₂-even composite Φ = ⟨ŝ²⟩ admits a derivative
expansion

    Γ_k[Φ]  =  ∫ d^d x [ (K_k(Φ)/2) (∇Φ)²  +  V_k(Φ) ]  +  O(∂⁴)

Write K_k(Φ) as a field-expansion

    K_k(Φ)  =  Σ_{n ≥ 0}  K_k^{(n)} · Φⁿ .

TGP target (H_GL in Φ-variable): K_*(Φ) = K_*^{(1)} · Φ with
K_*^{(0)} = 0 at the IR fixed point.

## 2. Ising 3D Wilson–Fisher data

Conformal-bootstrap values for the 3D Ising CFT (Kos–Poland–
Simmons-Duffin 2016 and subsequent):

    Δ_σ  =  0.518149(10)
    Δ_ε  =  1.412625(10)
    Δ_ε' =  3.82968(23)
    η    =  0.036298(20)
    ν    =  0.629971(4)

The scalar primaries relevant here are:

- σ (Z₂-odd, spin): `ŝ ↔ σ`
- ε (Z₂-even, energy): `[ŝ²] ↔ ε`   (normal-ordered composite)

Identification: `Φ = ⟨ŝ²⟩` corresponds to the ε operator, hence has
scaling dimension Δ_Φ = Δ_ε.

## 3. Scaling dimensions of candidate kinetic operators

Operator `O_n ≡ Φⁿ · (∇Φ)²` is a spin-0 descendant of
`ε^{n+1} · (∂ε)²`-type composites, with leading scaling dimension

    Δ(O_n)  ≈  (n+2) · Δ_ε  +  2 ,

where the `+2` comes from the two explicit spatial derivatives and
the `(n+2)` from `n` factors of Φ plus 2 from (∇Φ)². Corrections from
OPE subleading descendants are of order ≤ 10⁻² and will not change
the conclusions.

| n | Operator        | Δ(O_n)   | d − Δ(O_n) in d=3 | Verdict      |
|---|-----------------|----------|-------------------|--------------|
| 0 | (∇Φ)²           | 4.825    | −1.825            | irrelevant   |
| 1 | Φ (∇Φ)²         | 6.238    | −3.238            | irrelevant   |
| 2 | Φ² (∇Φ)²        | 7.650    | −4.650            | irrelevant   |
| 3 | Φ³ (∇Φ)²        | 9.063    | −6.063            | irrelevant   |

All candidate kinetic operators are **irrelevant** at 3D Ising WF.

## 4. Which irrelevant operator wins in the IR?

Under RG running, a coefficient of dimension (d − Δ_O) decays in the
IR as k^{d − Δ_O}. With all exponents negative, all coefficients decay,
but *the least-irrelevant one decays slowest*.

Ratios in the IR:

    K_k^{(1)} / K_k^{(0)}  ~  k^{Δ_ε}        =  k^{1.41}  →  0
    K_k^{(2)} / K_k^{(0)}  ~  k^{2Δ_ε}       =  k^{2.83}  →  0
    K_k^{(1)} / K_k^{(2)}  ~  k^{−Δ_ε}        =  k^{−1.41}  →  ∞

Therefore, at the IR fixed point:

    K_*(Φ)  ∝  K_*^{(0)}  +  (subleading corrections)

i.e. **K_*(Φ) → const**, not K_*(Φ) ∝ Φ.

This is the rigorous scaling-dimension argument that H₁ does not
generate H_GL at 3D Ising WF. It is consistent with the M2-a §2.v.b
observation (Δ_ε ≠ 2Δ_σ) and with the M2-b 1D numerical result.

## 5. Could a "miraculous cancellation" save M1-B?

For M1-B to close via RG, one would need the UV coefficient K_Λ^{(0)}
to vanish (or flow to zero faster than naively) while K_Λ^{(1)} remains
nonzero. Possibilities:

### 5.1 Symmetry-induced zero of K_Λ^{(0)} at UV

For H₁: the tree-level continuum of −J ŝ_iŝ_j is `(J a^{2−d}/2)(∇ŝ)²`.
Transforming to Φ = ŝ²:

    (∇ŝ)²  =  (∇Φ)² / (4Φ)
    ⇒  K_Φ^{tree}(Φ)  =  (J a^{2−d}/2) · 1/Φ .

At tree level, H₁ generates the operator `Φ^{−1}(∇Φ)²` (equivalently
ŝ-canonical kinetic), i.e. K_Φ^{(−1)} ≠ 0 but K_Φ^{(0)} = 0 at tree
level. So at the UV, K_Φ^{(0)} *does* vanish — it is not in the bare
action.

However, under RG, K_Φ^{(0)} is generated by one-loop corrections
(and, more importantly, by the non-polynomial Φ-structure generated
by integrating ŝ out at fixed Φ-profile). For standard LPA' in Ising
universality this generation is at O(1) scale at the UV; by the IR
fixed point it is order of the natural units. There is no known Ising
symmetry that forces K_Φ^{(0)} to vanish beyond tree level.

### 5.2 Parametric smallness

If K_Φ^{(0)}/K_Φ^{(1)} is parametrically small at some intermediate
scale, it might remain numerically small throughout the flow, making
K_Φ^{(1)} dominate in practice. This would be a dynamical accident,
not a symmetry-protected statement. M2-b 1D MC already rules this
out in 1D (p = −0.28, not +1); 3D would have to behave qualitatively
differently. Given matching universality structure, unlikely.

### 5.3 Dangerously irrelevant

An irrelevant operator is "dangerously irrelevant" if its coefficient
enters a physical quantity through a singular limit that amplifies
it. For stiffness/kinetic prefactors, no standard mechanism generates
such dangerous IR enhancement. Irrelevant stays irrelevant in IR
observables unless tuning is imposed.

### 5.4 Verdict

At standard 3D Ising WF, without additional ingredients, the leading
K_Φ^{(0)} operator dominates K_Φ^{(1)} by a factor `(Λ/k)^{Δ_ε} ≈
(Λ/k)^{1.41}` in the IR. For k/Λ = 10^{−3} (typical block-RG window),
suppression factor ≈ 10^{−4.2} — four orders of magnitude. There is
no realistic mechanism within Ising universality that removes this.

**Analytically, M1-B (pure H₁ at 3D Ising WF → H_GL) is ruled out.**

## 6. What minimal Z₂-symmetric extension could rescue M1-B?

The user's constraint: preserve Z₂-odd ŝ and "nothingness + Z₂"
emergence. What extensions of H₁ keep this and could land on H_GL?

### 6.1 Tricritical Ising (sextic term)

    H₁' = H₁  +  g · Σ_i ŝ_i⁶  (with λ tuned to zero)

At d=3, ŝ⁶ is at its upper critical dimension (tricritical Ising is
Gaussian-like at d=3). Δ_ε^{tri} = 1 (mean-field). Recompute:

| n | Δ(O_n)^{tri} | d − Δ(O_n) | Verdict   |
|---|--------------|------------|-----------|
| 0 | 4            | −1         | irrelevant|
| 1 | 5            | −2         | irrelevant|
| 2 | 6            | −3         | irrelevant|

Ratio K^{(1)}/K^{(0)} ~ k^1 still → 0 in IR. K^{(0)} still dominates.
**Tricritical point does not help.**

### 6.2 Higher-derivative kinetic term

    H₁' = H₁  +  g · Σ_i (∂²ŝ_i)²

Higher-derivative operators are more irrelevant than `(∂ŝ)²` and do
not modify Ising universality. **No help.**

### 6.3 Multi-component fields (O(N))

    ŝ → (ŝ_1, …, ŝ_N)  with O(N) symmetry  ⊃  Z₂

Δ_ε^{O(N)} approaches 2 from below as N → ∞. For large N:

    Δ(O_0) → 6,  d − Δ(O_0) = −3  (more irrelevant)
    Δ(O_1) → 8,  d − Δ(O_1) = −5

The ratio K^{(1)}/K^{(0)} becomes worse at large N, not better.
**O(N) extension does not help.**

### 6.4 Long-range interaction

    H₁' = H₁  −  Δ J · Σ_{i,j}  |i − j|^{−(d+σ)}  ŝ_i ŝ_j

For σ below a critical σ*, universality changes (long-range Ising);
Δ_ε(σ) varies continuously. At some σ we could in principle force
Δ_ε very large, making Δ(O_0) > d and O_0 relevant — but that breaks
the irrelevance hierarchy in a way that inverts the argument but
doesn't obviously give K^{(1)} dominance either. Also, long-range
interactions break locality, which compromises "nicość" as a natural
UV state (no obvious mechanism for `r^{-d-σ}` to emerge from an
empty substrate). **Weakens the ontological narrative.**

### 6.5 Auxiliary Z₂-even density field (two-field model)

    H₁'  =  H₁(ŝ)  +  g · Σ_i  ŝ_i²  χ_i  +  (1/2) Σ_i  χ_i²
                  +  J_χ · Σ_{⟨ij⟩}  (χ_i − χ_j)²

Integrate out χ (Gaussian), effective action:

    H_eff(ŝ)  =  H₁(ŝ)  −  (g²/2) Σ_i  (ŝ²)²_i  +  bond couplings

The induced bond coupling, at leading order in `g²/J_χ`, is

    −  (g² / (2 J_χ))  ·  Σ_{⟨ij⟩}  (ŝ²)_i · (ŝ²)_j  +  O(g⁴) ,

which is **exactly form A (H₃ bilinear)** we already ruled out in
M2-c — and therefore does not give α = 2. Two-field extension via
this route does not help.

### 6.6 Gauged Z₂ (Ising gauge theory)

Introduce Z₂ gauge link variables. Changes universality entirely
(confinement / deconfinement). Breaks the "smooth continuum limit"
philosophy. **Different theory altogether.**

## 7. Non-perturbative escape?

Possibilities that evade the CFT argument:

- **Dualities**: in 2D Ising, disorder operators (duality-related)
  have different scaling. In 3D Ising no exact duality; but
  approximate "Ising dual" in continuum is Ising gauge theory
  (section 6.6). Not usable.
- **Topological sector**: instanton-mediated coarse-graining could
  in principle bypass CFT. In 3D Ising no known instantons of the
  required structure. Speculative.
- **Non-commutative geometry on the substrate**: radical; breaks
  locality.

None of these is a concrete route at present.

## 8. Gate verdict

The analytical argument is clean:

1. At 3D Ising WF, `K_Φ(Φ) → const` in IR, not `K_Φ(Φ) ∝ Φ`.
2. Simple Z₂-symmetric extensions of H₁ (tricritical, higher-
   derivative, O(N), long-range, two-field) do not change this.
3. Only non-perturbative / symmetry-radical escapes remain, and
   they either break the "Z₂ + nothingness" narrative (§6.4, §6.6)
   or are speculative (§7).

**M1-B is analytically disfavoured at 3D Ising WF.**

This does not yet close M1-B: M3-a (1D block-RG with bond-form
measurement) and M3-b (3D MC cluster simulation with block-RG) are
the numerical gates that could overrule this analytical prediction
in the unlikely event of a surprise. But the prior on M1-B closing
positively should now be lowered from "< 50%" to **"< 5%"**.
