  Matter coupling: f(Phi, sigma) ~ sqrt(g_tt^GR/g_tt^TGP)
  Mechanism: sigma sources GR-like backbone, decouples in weak-field

  Cost (a) Strong-field rescue: PLAUSIBLE

      Box sigma = beta × T^mat_mu^mu (sourced by matter)
    - tunable to mimic scenario (e) at photon ring

  Cost (b) Weak-field PPN: STRINGENT (Brans-Dicke)
    - In Brans-Dicke: γ_PPN = (1+ω)/(2+ω) where ω is BD coupling
    - Cassini bound |γ_PPN-1| < 2.3e-5 → ω > 40000
    - Strong constraint: sigma must be heavy or screened
    - Vainshtein/chameleon screening required

  Cost (c) Structural risk: HIGH
    - Violates single-Φ Z_2 substrate principle
    - Two-field potential V(Φ,σ) introduces tuning
    - Requires explanation: why two fields?

  Verdict A: VIABLE but expensive structurally

[T-M92.5] CANDIDATE D: Momentum back-reaction
  Action: S = S_M9.1'' + α ∫ T^μν J_μ J_ν √-g d⁴x
  with J_μ = ∂_μ Φ (substrate flow)
  Mechanism: stress-energy back-reaction self-suppresses Φ in strong-field

  Cost (a) Strong-field rescue: PLAUSIBLE
    - In strong-field T^μν is large, J_μ J^μ is large → α × T·J·J term grows
    - Effective Φ-EOM gets modified by back-reaction term
    - Matches Lenz-law intuition (substrate resists curvature)

  Cost (b) Weak-field PPN: AUTO-PASS
    - In weak-field T^μν ~ U², J_μ ~ U, so T·J·J ~ U^4
    - Negligible vs leading O(U) terms → γ_PPN, β_PPN preserved at 1PN
    - Naturally activates only in strong-field (no fine-tuning)

  Cost (c) Structural risk: MEDIUM-HIGH
    - Non-linear in T^μν → ghost modes if α has wrong sign
    - Higher-derivative terms after integration by parts
    - Unitarity analysis required (Ostrogradsky instability check)
    - But: physically motivated (substrate self-back-reaction)

  Verdict D: PROMISING — natural strong-field activation,
             auto-preserves weak-field, but stability nontrivial
