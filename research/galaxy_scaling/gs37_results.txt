==============================================================================
  PART A: ROTATION CURVE MODELS
==============================================================================

  A.1  Model components
  ──────────────────────
  Baryonic mass model:
    - Exponential disk: M(<R) = M_d * [1 - (1+R/R_d) exp(-R/R_d)]
    - Gas disk: same form with scale length 2*R_d (gas more extended)
    - M/L_disk as free parameter (typically 0.3-0.8 at 3.6 um)

  TGP rotation velocity:
    V_TGP^2(R) = V_bar^2(R) * nu(g_bar/a0)
    where nu(y) = 1 + exp(-y^(4/5)) / y^gamma
    For disk galaxies: c_eff=1, gamma=0.400

  MOND rotation velocity:
    V_MOND^2(R) = V_bar^2(R) * nu_MOND(g_bar/a0)
    where nu_MOND(y) = (1 + sqrt(1+4/y)) / 2

  C.1  Building the RAR from rotation curves
  ────────────────────────────────────────────
  For each galaxy, at each radius R:
    g_obs = V_obs^2(R) / R    (observed centripetal acceleration)
    g_bar = V_bar^2(R) / R    (baryonic, from photometry)

  The RAR is: g_obs vs g_bar, or equivalently nu = g_obs/g_bar vs y = g_bar/a0

  D.2  Where TGP diverges from MOND
  ───────────────────────────────────

  At HIGH y (y > 1): TGP screening sharper than MOND
    - TGP: exp(-y^(4/5)) kills the correction FAST
    - MOND: 1/y correction dies as power law
    - At y=10: TGP excess = 0.1%, MOND excess = 9.2%
    - OBSERVABLE: inner rotation curves of HSB galaxies

  At INTERMEDIATE y (y ~ 0.1-1.0): THE KEY REGIME
    - TGP nu_disk(0.3) = 2.11, MOND nu(0.3) = 2.39
    - Difference: ~12%
    - This is the transition region: most sensitive
    - SPARC galaxies populate this range densely

  At LOW y (y < 0.01): gamma effect
    - TGP(c=1): nu ~ 1/y^0.4 → flatter than MOND (1/y^0.5)
    - At y=0.01: TGP = 7.15, MOND = 10.51 → TGP 32% lower!
    - OBSERVABLE: outer rotation curves of LSB galaxies
    - This is where M/L fitting compensates

==============================================================================
  PART F: DISK THICKNESS AND c_eff
==============================================================================

  F.1  The thickness-codimension connection
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  Thin disk (h/R << 1): effectively 2D → c_eff = 1 (gamma=0.400)
  Thick disk (h/R ~ 0.3): intermediate → c_eff = 1.5 (gamma=0.480)
  Spheroidal (h/R ~ 1): effectively 3D → c_eff = 2 (gamma=0.533)

  Mapping: c_eff = 1 + (h/R) * 2  (linear interpolation)
  Or more physical: c_eff = 1 + 2*(h/R)^2 for small h/R

    McGaugh RAR:            chi2/dof = 19.52

  G.2  Key findings
  ──────────────────

  1. TGP with c_eff=1 (pure thin disk) is slightly WORSE than MOND
     - Reason: gamma=0.4 gives less boost at low y than MOND (gamma_eff=0.5)
     - M/L fitting partially compensates but not fully

  2. DISK THICKNESS matters:
     - Real galaxies have h/R ~ 0.05-0.25 → c_eff ~ 1.1-1.5
     - c_eff = 1.3 (gamma=0.452) is very close to MOND (0.5)
     - This is physically motivated: no fine-tuning

  3. HSB vs LSB:
     - HSB: TGP ~ MOND (both near-Newtonian)
     - LSB: TGP(c=1) < MOND by ~10% at V_flat
     - LSB disks are typically thicker → c_eff > 1 → gap closes

  4. SHARP SCREENING:
     - TGP at y>3: correction < 5% (exponential screening)
     - MOND at y>3: correction ~ 26% (power-law)
     - Inner rotation curves of HSB favor TGP-like screening

  G.3  Predictions
  ──────────────────
  1. RAR residuals should correlate with disk h/R ratio
     → Thick disks: higher nu_obs at given y (higher c_eff)
     → Thin disks: lower nu_obs (c_eff closer to 1)
     → SPARC has no h/R data, but edge-on galaxies constrain this

  2. The transition region (y ~ 0.3-1.0) is most diagnostic
     → TGP gives SHARPER transition than MOND
     → Need: galaxies with many points in this range

  3. Deep MOND regime (y < 0.01):
     → TGP(c=1): V ~ M^(1/(2(1-gamma))) = M^0.833 (BTFR slope ~3.3)
     → MOND:     V ~ M^(1/4) (BTFR slope 4.0)
     → TGP(c=1.3): slope ~ 3.7 — closer to observed ~3.8-4.0
     → THE BTFR SLOPE CONSTRAINS c_eff for disks!

  G.4  BTFR slope constraint on c_eff
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  In deep MOND: V^4 ~ G*M*a0 → slope = 4.0
  In deep TGP:  V^(2/(1-gamma)) ~ M → slope = 2/(1-gamma)
