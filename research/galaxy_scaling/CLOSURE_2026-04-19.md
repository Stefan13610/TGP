**Bottom line:** TGP works as a ν(y)-parameterized modified-gravity theory with one free constant a₀. This is **Path B** functionality. It is not worse than MOND/AQUAL in any empirical test; it is not better as a first-principles theory.

## 3. What does not work (rigorously established no-go)

### 3a. gs65 — Static TGP derivation (formal)

- **Linearized**: `∇²φ − γφ = S` → Yukawa (exp-damped) or tachyonic (oscillatory cos). **Neither is log(r).**
- **Weak nonlinearity**: vacuum ODE `ψ'' + 2ψ'/r + α(ψ')²/ψ = 0` admits power-law solutions `ψ ~ r^(-1/3)` (sympy-verified).
- **Rotation curve consequence**: g ~ r^(−4/3), v ~ r^(−1/6). **Falls faster than Newton**, opposite to MOND.
- **Potential U(ψ)**: U''(1) = −γ < 0 (slow-roll max). No natural oscillation frequency → **Mech F definitively refuted**.
- **AQUAL comparison**: TGP kinetic `α(∇Φ)²/Φ` is not of form F((∇Φ)²); cannot reduce to L~(∇Φ)³ in any limit.

### 3b. gs66 — Linear FRW propagator (no-go theorem)

- **Quasi-static propagator**: `G̃(k) = 1/(k²/a² − γ + 3iH²)`, complex-mass Yukawa.
- **Closed-form real-space**: `G(r) = exp(−μr)/(4πr)` with `μ = sqrt(−γ+3iH²)`.
- **Behavior**: Newton (1/r) for r<<L_nat; oscillatory/damped for r>>L_nat; **never log(r)**.
- **Fourier-power theorem**: log(r) far-field ↔ `G̃(k) ~ 1/k³` at k→0. Every polynomial D(k) from sek02 yields `G̃ → const` or `G̃ → 1/k²`. **No linear TGP extension can produce MOND.**
- **Numerical verification** across L_nat ∈ {3 kpc, L_H, c/H₀}: all scenarios give Newton→screened, never flat rotation curves for M=1.5×10¹¹ M☉.

### 3c. What remains (for potential revival, not on critical path)

- **Nonlinear FRW dynamics** (not computed in this program). Given the static result `ψ~r^(−1/3)`, nonlinearity unlikely to produce log(r). Not excluded, but not a priority.
- **Theory extensions** (Bridge b dual-scale, Bridge c smooth membrane) — possible but require new axioms, removing the "unique derivation" claim regardless.

## 4. Path C decision rationale

Three paths were offered in gs66 verdict:

| Path | What it preserves | What it costs |
|---|---|---|
| A (extension) | MOND in TGP | One extra axiom; unique-derivation claim gone |
| B (phenomenology) | Fit quality | Theoretical status = "consistent with MOND" only |
| **C (closure)** | **Theoretical honesty; predictive focus** | **Galaxy as TGP test abandoned** |

**Path C chosen.** Rationale:
- TGP has **multiple independently strong** predictions in other domains (lepton masses to 0.013%, Koide to 0.0006%, k=4 exactly, N=3 generations). These do NOT depend on galaxy-MOND matching.
- Forcing galaxy-MOND as a TGP prediction would require ad-hoc extensions that weaken the overall theoretical parsimony.
- Competing alternatives (MOND/AQUAL/QUMOND) also lack first-principles derivation of a₀; TGP is no worse, but claiming uniqueness is overreach.
- The numerical coincidence a₀ = c·H₀/(2π) is preserved as an **empirical observation**, to be explained by future work if possible.

## 7. Lessons

1. **Formal derivation BEFORE extensive phenomenology**. gs1-gs49 fit data well; gs65/gs66 show this was parameter-space phenomenology, not TGP-unique derivation. Future programs should attempt formal derivation first to avoid post-hoc rationalization.
2. **Numerical coincidences can mislead**. a₀ = c·H₀/(2π) is 87% accurate but does not follow from TGP. Dimensional analysis is necessary, not sufficient.
3. **Falsification theorems are valuable**. The Fourier-power no-go (gs66) is a clean analytical result — more decisive than any number of chi² comparisons.
4. **Theoretical parsimony matters**. Bridges (b) and (c) would "save" MOND at the cost of axiom count. Path C preserves parsimony at the cost of abandoning one target. This is the correct trade for a theory with other strong predictions.
