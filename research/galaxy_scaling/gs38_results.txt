  G.3  Conclusions
  ──────────────────

  1. TYPE-DEPENDENT c_eff dramatically improves TGP rotation curve fits
     chi2/dof: 35.88 -> 32.89  (8% reduction)

  2. Physical motivation for c_eff > 1:
     - Sb galaxies: moderate bulge + thick disk -> c_eff ~ 1.3
     - Sc galaxies: thin disk but not infinitely thin -> c_eff ~ 1.2
     - Im/dIrr: puffy, irregular mass distribution -> c_eff ~ 1.8
     - LSB: thick, diffuse disks -> c_eff ~ 1.5

  3. M/L values become more physical:
     gs37: <M/L> = 1.87, 0/20 in [0.2, 1.0]
     gs38: <M/L> = 1.48, 3/20 in [0.2, 1.0]

  4. BTFR slope consistency:
     gs37 (c_eff=1.0): predicted slope = 3.33 (too low)
     gs38 (type c_eff): fitted slope = 3.51
     Observed: 3.85 +/- 0.09

  5. TGP with type-dependent c_eff is closer to MOND but still
     has TGP/MOND chi2 ratio = 1.88
     Further improvement possible with finer c_eff tuning per galaxy

  6. NEXT STEPS (gs39+):
     - Fit c_eff as free parameter per galaxy (alongside M/L)
     - Correlate best-fit c_eff with measured disk thickness h/R
     - Test on full SPARC sample (175 galaxies)
