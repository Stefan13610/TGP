  A.1  The Wolf mass estimator
  ─────────────────────────────
  For a dispersion-supported system (Walker+ 2009, Wolf+ 2010):

    M_1/2 = 4 * sigma_los^2 * r_half / G

  where M_1/2 = mass within the 3D half-light radius r_1/2 ~ 4/3 r_half
  This is remarkably model-independent.

  In TGP: M_1/2 = M_bar(r_1/2) * nu(y)
  where y = G M_bar(r_1/2) / (r_1/2^2 * a0)

  Assuming M/L_* ~ 2 (typical for old stellar populations):
    M_bar = 2 * L_V

  B.2  The ultra-faint challenge
  ────────────────────────────────
  Ultra-faints have y ~ 10^-5 to 10^-3.
  At these y values:
    y=1e-05: nu_c2=465.1, nu_c3=1000.9, nu_MOND=316.7
    y=1e-04: nu_c2=136.8, nu_c3=252.0, nu_MOND=100.5
    y=1e-03: nu_c2=40.7, nu_c3=63.8, nu_MOND=32.1
    y=1e-02: nu_c2=12.4, nu_c3=16.5, nu_MOND=10.5

  TGP nu grows as ~1/y^gamma at low y (power-law)
  MOND nu grows as ~1/sqrt(y) at low y
  For c=2: gamma=0.533, faster than MOND (0.5)
  For c=3: gamma=0.600, even faster
  → TGP SHOULD do better than MOND for ultra-faints!

==============================================================================
  PART C: EXTERNAL FIELD EFFECT (EFE)
==============================================================================

  C.1  EFE in TGP
  ─────────────────
  dSphs orbit within the MW gravitational field.
  The external field g_ext from MW modifies the internal dynamics.

  In MOND/AQUAL: if g_ext > g_int, the system becomes Newtonian
  (the boost is suppressed). This is the EFE.

  In TGP (f(R) formulation): the EFE is WEAKER than in MOND.
  Reason: f(R) screening depends on local curvature R, not |nabla Phi|.
  The scalaron phi_s responds to R = R_int + R_ext, but the
  nonlinear mixing is different from AQUAL.

  Approximate TGP-EFE:
    nu_eff(y_int, y_ext) ~ nu(sqrt(y_int^2 + y_ext^2))
  vs MOND-EFE:
    nu_eff(y_int, y_ext) ~ nu(y_int + y_ext)

  C.4  EFE summary
  ──────────────────
  TGP EFE (quadrature): WEAKER than MOND EFE (linear)
  → For nearby dSphs (D<100 kpc): y_ext ~ 0.1-0.5 → significant
  → For distant dSphs (D>200 kpc): y_ext < 0.02 → EFE negligible
  → TGP advantage: weaker EFE avoids the over-suppression problem
     that MOND has for some classical dSphs (e.g., Fornax)

  In gs32, we derived: gamma = alpha * c / (c+1)

==============================================================================
  PART F: THE MASS-SIGMA RELATION
==============================================================================

  F.1  Theoretical predictions
  ──────────────────────────────
  In the deep MOND regime (y << 1):
    MOND: g_obs ~ sqrt(g_N * a0) → M ~ sigma^4 / (G*a0)
    TGP:  g_obs ~ g_N^(1-gamma) * a0^gamma ~ g_N * y^(-gamma)

  For dSphs (assuming virial equilibrium):
    sigma^2 ~ G*M_eff/r = G*M_bar*nu/r
    In deep MOND: nu ~ 1/y^gamma ~ (r^2*a0/(G*M_bar))^gamma
    → sigma^2 ~ G*M_bar/r * (r^2*a0/(G*M_bar))^gamma
    → sigma^2 ~ G^(1-gamma) * M_bar^(1-gamma) * r^(2*gamma-1) * a0^gamma

  For MOND (gamma=0.5): sigma^4 ~ G*M_bar*a0 (Tully-Fisher-like)
  For TGP c=2.5 (gamma=0.571): sigma^(2/(1-gamma)) ~ scaling

  F.3  Key observations
  ──────────────────────
  - dSphs do NOT follow the BTFR (M ~ sigma^4)

==============================================================================
  PART G: SUMMARY -- DWARF SPHEROIDALS IN TGP
==============================================================================

  G.1  Key findings
  ──────────────────

  1. CLASSICAL dSphs (M/L ~ 10-600):
     - TGP with c_eff=2.5 (gamma=0.571) gives reasonable sigma_los
     - Performance comparable to MOND
     - Fornax, Leo I: well predicted
     - High M/L objects (UMi, Sextans): challenging for both TGP and MOND

  2. ULTRA-FAINT dSphs (M/L > 100):
     - TGP gamma=0.571 > MOND 0.5 → MORE boost at very low y
     - This is an ADVANTAGE: ultra-faints need high M/L
     - But: some ultra-faints (Segue 1, Tucana II) need M/L > 1000
     - Even TGP may need additional physics (tidal effects, binaries)

  3. EXTERNAL FIELD EFFECT:
     - TGP EFE is WEAKER than MOND EFE (quadrature vs linear)
     - This HELPS for nearby dSphs where MOND EFE over-suppresses
     - Fornax: MOND+EFE too low sigma, TGP+EFE closer to observed

  4. MORPHOLOGY:
     - dSphs are oblate spheroids → c_eff ~ 2-3
     - From ellipticities: c_eff ~ 2.2-2.6, mean ~ 2.5
     - TGP predicts gamma(c_eff) → different nu for each dSph
     - TESTABLE: round dSphs (low eps) should have higher sigma
       at fixed M_bar and r_half than elongated ones

  G.2  TGP vs MOND scorecard for dSphs
  ──────────────────────────────────────

    | Aspect                  | TGP c=2.5 | MOND    |
    |─────────────────────────|───────────|─────────|
    | Classical dSphs         | ~OK       | ~OK     |
    | Ultra-faint (low M/L)   | Better    | OK      |
    | Ultra-faint (high M/L)  | Problem   | Problem |

  G.3  Predictions for future observations
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  1. ROUND dSphs (eps<0.15) should have ~10-15% higher sigma
     than ELONGATED dSphs (eps>0.35) at fixed M_bar, r_half
     → Testable with spectroscopic surveys (DESI, 4MOST)

  2. Isolated dSphs (far from MW/M31) should show FULL TGP boost
     → No EFE suppression → higher sigma than MOND predicts
     → Examples: Leo T, Phoenix II, Eridanus II

  3. Tidal dSphs (e.g. Sgr): TGP phantom DM halo is LESS bound
     than LCDM subhalo → different tidal stripping signature
     → Stream morphology test: TGP streams should be WIDER
