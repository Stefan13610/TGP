SECTION C: Radial Acceleration Relation (RAR) Shift with z
==============================================================================

The RAR: g_obs = nu(g_bar/a0) * g_bar
With evolving a0, the transition from Newtonian to deep-MOND shifts.

    z    a0(z) [m/s^2]  log10(a0)   Transition g_bar  Shift [dex]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
  0.0       1.2000e-10    -9.9208         ~1.20e-10       0.0000
  0.5       1.3781e-10    -9.8607         ~1.38e-10       0.0601
  1.0       1.8662e-10    -9.7290         ~1.87e-10       0.1918
  2.0       3.1607e-10    -9.5002         ~3.16e-10       0.4206

Key result: At z=1, the RAR transition shifts by 0.192 dex in g_bar.
This means the deep-MOND regime begins at HIGHER accelerations at z=1.

Interpretation:
  At low g_bar (deep MOND): g_obs = sqrt(g_bar * a0)
  -> g_obs(z=1)/g_obs(z=0) = sqrt(a0(z=1)/a0(z=0))
  = sqrt(1.5552) = 1.2471
  At high g_bar (Newtonian): g_obs ~ g_bar, no change.

SECTION F: Theory Comparison
==============================================================================

+-------------------+------------------+----------------------------+
| Theory            | a0(z) prediction | BTFR zeropoint at z=1      |
+-------------------+------------------+----------------------------+
| MOND (Milgrom)    | a0 = const       | No shift (0%)              |
| LCDM              | No a0 concept    | No specific prediction     |
| TGP               | a0 = cH(z)/2pi   | +11.7% in V_flat            |
| Verlinde (2017)   | a0 ~ cH           | Similar to TGP (~+11.7%)    |
| f(R) gravity      | Model-dependent  | Varies (0% to ~20%)        |
| MSTG (Moffat)     | a0 ~ const       | ~0% (no evolution)         |
+-------------------+------------------+----------------------------+

Key distinctions:
  MOND vs TGP: a0 = const vs a0 ~ H(z)
    -> At z=1: MOND predicts SAME BTFR, TGP predicts 11.7% shift
  LCDM vs TGP: LCDM has no prediction for RAR evolution
    -> TGP makes a SPECIFIC, TESTABLE prediction
  Verlinde vs TGP: Both predict a0 ~ H, but differ in details
    -> Verlinde: a0 = cH/(2*pi) * f(geometry)
    -> TGP: a0 = cH/(2*pi) exactly

Note on LCDM:
  In LCDM, the RAR is an 'emergent' correlation from galaxy formation.
  Its evolution depends on baryonic physics (feedback, gas fractions).
  No clean prediction for BTFR zeropoint evolution exists.
  Any observed shift would need to be compared to simulations (FIRE, EAGLE).
