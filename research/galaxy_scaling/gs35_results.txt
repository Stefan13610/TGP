  PART A: LENSING SHEAR PROFILES ΔΣ(R)
==============================================================================

  A.1  Lensing observables
  ─────────────────────────
  Weak lensing measures the TANGENTIAL SHEAR γ_t(R):
    γ_t(R) = ΔΣ(R) / Σ_cr

  where ΔΣ(R) = Σ̄(<R) - Σ(R) is the EXCESS surface density:
    Σ̄(<R) = (2/R²) ∫₀ᴿ Σ(R') R' dR'  (mean within R)
    Σ(R) = ∫₋∞^∞ ρ(√(R²+z²)) dz       (projected density)

  KEY: In TGP, Σ = ν(y) × Σ_bar  (no cancellation!)
  In ΛCDM:  Σ = Σ_bar + Σ_DM     (NFW profile)

  For lensing: Σ_TGP and Σ_ΛCDM give DIFFERENT radial shapes.

  B.1  TGP unique prediction: γ depends on Sérsic index
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  In TGP: γ = α × c_eff/(c_eff + 1)
    Disk galaxies (c_eff=1):       γ = 0.400
    Elliptical galaxies (c_eff=2):  γ = 0.533
    BCGs / clusters (c_eff=3):      γ = 0.600

  This means: at the SAME baryonic mass and radius,
  an elliptical has MORE phantom DM than a disk galaxy!

  In ΛCDM: galaxy-galaxy lensing depends on halo mass,
  NOT on galaxy morphology (at fixed stellar mass).

  B.3  TGP signature: elliptical/disk ΔΣ ratio
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────
  At fixed M_stars, TGP predicts:
    ΔΣ_elliptical / ΔΣ_disk ≈ y^(γ_disk - γ_ell) = y^(-0.133)
  → Larger difference at LOW y (large R)
  → At R=500 kpc: ~20-40% more signal for ellipticals

  ΛCDM prediction: ratio depends ONLY on M_halo(morphology)
  → Any morphology dependence is indirect (stellar-to-halo mass relation)
  → SHMR difference at fixed M*: ~0.2 dex → ~60% in ΔΣ

  TEST: Bin galaxies by Sérsic index n at fixed M*.
  TGP: ΔΣ(n>2.5) / ΔΣ(n<1.5) at R>200 kpc should show
  CONTINUOUS increase with n, not a step function.
  ΛCDM: step function (disk halos vs elliptical halos).

  Combined with photometry: g_bar(R) = G M_bar(R) / R²
