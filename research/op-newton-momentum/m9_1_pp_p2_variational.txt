[P2-B] Conformal invariance
==============================================================================

  TGP's V(Phi) = (gamma/3) Phi^3/Phi_0 - (gamma/4) Phi^4/Phi_0^2.
  Cubic term Phi^3/Phi_0 has conformal weight 3, not 4.
  Hence V is NOT 4D-conformal-invariant (under Phi -> lambda Phi).
  Strict conformal-invariance principle FAILS.
  Verdict: cannot derive f from this principle alone.

[P2-C] Substrate-budget extension: f vanishes at second zero of V
==============================================================================

  Postulates:
    (E1) f(1) = 1            [vacuum calibration]
    (E2) f(4/3) = 0          [vanishes at second zero of V]
    (E3) f -> infty at psi=0 [non-metric phase boundary]
    (E4) f rational of minimal degree compatible with (E1)-(E3)

  Try f(psi) = (a + b psi)/psi  [rational, pole at psi=0, simple zero]
  Conditions (E1)+(E2) give: {a: 4, b: -3}
  Result:    f(psi) = -3 + 4/psi
  Factored:  f(psi) = -(3*psi - 4)/psi

  Compare: V(psi)/Phi^4 normalized to vacuum (P2-D below):
           f = -(3*psi - 4)/psi
  P2-C and P2-D agree:  True

==============================================================================
[P2-D] Dimensional naturalness: dimensionless V/Phi^n combinations
==============================================================================

  In natural units: [Phi]=mass, [V]=mass^4.  Dimensionless ratios:
        V/Phi^4  =  gamma*(4 - 3*psi)/(12*Phi_0**2*psi)
     normalized  =  -(3*psi - 4)/psi

       V'/Phi^3  =  gamma*(1 - psi)/(Phi_0**2*psi)    (vanishes at psi=1, can't normalize)

      V''/Phi^2  =  gamma*(2 - 3*psi)/(Phi_0**2*psi)
     normalized  =  (3*psi - 2)/psi

       V'''/Phi  =  2*gamma*(1 - 3*psi)/(Phi_0**2*psi)
     normalized  =  (3*psi - 1)/(2*psi)

  V/Phi^4 normalized:
    f(psi) = -(3*psi - 4)/psi

  This is THE LOWEST-DERIVATIVE dimensionless combination of V, Phi.
  All higher-derivative ratios (V'/Phi^3, V''/Phi^2, V'''/Phi) involve
  more substrate physics (gradients, masses, couplings), so they
  are NOT minimal-complexity choices.

  Verdict: P2-D PICKS V/Phi^4 uniquely as the simplest
  dimensionless substrate ratio for time-dilation factor.
