Conclusion: the constraint f''(1) = f'(1)(f'(1)+2) with alpha=2 and f*h=1
is a SINGLE EQUATION in TWO Taylor coefficients of f.

Hence a 1-parameter family of admissible f exists. The QUESTION is whether
any such f has a NATURAL substrate-level derivation (substrate budget,
substrate action, conformal coupling, etc.).

Forms tested:
  (1) Pure power:        FAIL (only trivial p=0).
  (2) Exponential:       FAIL (only trivial a=0).
  (3) Rational:          a=-2 (pole psi=3/2, near basin boundary).
  (4) Hyperbolic:        a=+4 (zero at psi=4/3 = basin boundary).
  (5) Logarithmic:       always satisfies f'(1)=a, f''(1)=-a+2b;
                         constraint -a + 2b = a(a+2)  =>  b = (a^2+3a)/2.
                         For a=-1: b=-1; for a=-2: b=-1; for a=-3: b=0.
  (6) Quadratic Taylor:  by construction, infinite family.

Of these, only (4) shows a NATURAL connection to TGP physics:
  f(psi) = 1 + 4*(1-psi)/psi = (4 - 3*psi)/psi
  Zero precisely at psi = 4/3 -- the BASIN BOUNDARY of TGP
  (cf. action `eq:action-full-psi`, ghost-free range psi in (0, 4/3)).

metric choice, or a coincidence?
